An emotional closing page

When a bond stops feeling mutual.

This page carries the truth, the silence, the effort, and the final acceptance. It is written like a closure letter — soft, honest, and unforgettable.

The message

I trusted you.

Not casually… not normally. I trusted you in a way where you slowly became a part of my life — and then without even realising it, you became the centre of it.

And maybe that is exactly where I went wrong.

Because while I was getting closer, I could feel you going the opposite way.

I noticed your texts. The dryness. The distance. The lack of effort.

The things I did for you — the edits, the website, the time I invested — they were never for a “thank you”. They were my way of building something real.

But your replies, your behaviour, your consistency kept telling me one thing again and again: this bond does not exist for you the way it does for me.

What I felt

5 days of silence I stopped myself again and again, fighting the urge to just talk normally.
Deep attachment This was never casual for me. I was emotionally invested in a way that was hard to hide.
One-sided effort I was the one initiating, holding, trying, and carrying the bond forward.
Final understanding One-sided effort, no matter how pure, still ends up nowhere.

Acceptance

You will never know how difficult these last 5 days were for me. Not texting you. Holding back. Keeping myself from reaching out again and again.

I saw the pattern clearly: I was the one putting in effort while you kept everything limited. Even when I shared things that mattered to me, your responses felt empty.

Maybe I gave more than what you were ever meant to receive. And I know this clearly — it is going to take me time to forget this, to detach from you, because what I felt was real.

Now finally, our chapter is closing.

Take care, and thank you for all your memories.

Singing off with this — Swayam Mishra.
